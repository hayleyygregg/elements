Helium

Origin of the name: Helium was first discovered in the suns corona and got its name from the Greek word ‘helios’ which means sun.

Helium is the second largest element in the universe and the first in the family of noble gases. It is a simple element with one atomic orbital and is present in stars. Natural gas contains up to 7% Helium.

History

During a total eclipse in 1868, a new yellow line was observed by Pierre J. C. Janssen which indicated a new element. The same line was recorded by Joseph Norman Lockyer in London who was observing the sun. He assumed the new element to be a metal and named it Helium.

The same line was found again by Luigi Palmieri in a specturm of gases emitted by Volcano, Mount Vesuvius, located in Italy, in 1882.

American, William Hillebrand also discovered Helium when he collected the gas given off by the mineral uraninite (UO2) as it dissolved in acid in 1889. However, it was Per Teodor Cleve and Nils Abraham Langer at Uppsala, Sweden, in 1895, who repeated that experiment and confirmed it was helium and measured its atomic weight.
