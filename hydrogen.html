Hydrogen

Origin of the name: The name is derived from the Greek 'hydro' and 'genes' meaning water forming.

Hydrogen is the first element in the periodic table and the most basic and common of all elements in the Universe. Over 90% of all the atoms in the universe are hydrogen atoms, and they are the lightest of all elements.

History

The credit for the discovery of Hydrogen goes to Henry Cavendish who in 1766 collected bubbles given off during the process of adding iron filings to sulfuric acid and showed they were different from other gases.

He later showed that when Hydrogen burns it forms water, thereby ending the belief that water was an element.

In 1931 a rarer form of Hydrogen was discovered by Harold Urey and his colleagues at Columbia University, US. This has twice the mass of normal Hydrogen, and is named Deuterium.
